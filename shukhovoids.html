Shukhovoids : Signaled Dystopias
The Generic Sublime undertakes the project of radical integration of the skyscraper and takes it to the level of the ultimate post-urban megalomania: a complex building that organizes the territory in a synthetic field of competition and synergy. The Generic Sublime upgrades the organizational capabilities of the skyscraper by exploring the self-surmounting form that the anti-urban takes when assembled in a collective.

Spring 2013: The Generic Sublime, Utopioids, Professor Ciro Najle
This project begins with a study of Russian Constructivism as a utopian project with Vladimir Shukhov’s Radio Tower as a model of this utopia to be exploited and remodeled to its “post-urban” limits inherent in both it as a structure and general Leninist project in the creation of a “Utopioid” prototype. Research exposed that the utopian idealism involved in its conception ultimately led to its failure as a Leninist project; due to a lack of steel in Russia following the revolutions, the tower was ultimately built at less than half of its intended height, indeed shorter than the Eiffel, the Western structure against which the Shukhov tower was to be set.
