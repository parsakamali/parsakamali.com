Outlook : Peace Corps Commemorative Work 
A competition entry for the Peace Corps Commemorative Work in Washington D.C.
down
up
with Jennifer Ly and Jackie Park, June 2015
Outlook seeks to embody the ideals of the Peace Corps through the design of a living place that transfigures an open-ended, collective experience embedded in the everyday. Composed of two elements — an undulating path and an expansive groundscape — the Outlook is shaped by the relationship between the two and creates opportunities for exchange in the spaces between. This reciprocity recognizes one of the core tenets of the Peace Corps, that we live in a connected world where the effects of one action are experienced far beyond its origin point, and consequently, that powerful change can be effected one individual at a time.

The road towards peace is not always clearly defined, and for Peace Corps volunteers, the two-year journey is as meaningful as the act of reaching a final “destination.” The Outlook allows visitors to engage in this process of becoming as they embody this space of transformation. The curving path extends one’s experience through the site, the entire length of which is embedded with seating and a temporal narrative of the Peace Corps. Inflections in the path respond to eight newly planted trees and define the groundscape below. The path itself is expressed in copper, a material whose patina visually indexes the passage of time. The continual physical transformation of The Outlook embodies the ongoing nature of the Peace Corp’s mission, the pursuit of which affects us all.
