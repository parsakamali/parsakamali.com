Alloy Development

Real Estate Renter's Mobile App

A speculative product I designed at wireframe/medium-fidelity for a real estate development firm to think about how to address two of their potential mobile users: prospective renters and their current residents.

This prototype was designed to show potential flows for each of these users, in particular the prospective renter. It first shows how the curious renter can explore Alloy's properties, select and learn about a unit and directly apply for it from the app, all the way through running their credit.

The second part of the flow shows the potential a mobile app can have for Alloy's current renters by allowing them to track their energy usage, pay utilities, submit repair requests and deal directly with property managers.

Longtermists

Financial Podcast Platform

Prototype made with FramerJS
Product concepting/strategy and UX prototypes created at medium-fidelity for a very large financial institution looking to connect their brand with savvy individual investors. These prototypes illustrate two potential directions, both around the idea of creating a podcast from events that the institution will hold. The first prototype shows a "public" podcast, where the audio is displayed in a vertical format that allows users to comment on the event in "real-listening time" and create threads around specific topics. The second design illustrates a concept where the action around the podcast takes place in private by placing the user in a room with like-minded investors and feeds them relevant content to comment on and discuss.
