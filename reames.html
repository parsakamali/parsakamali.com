Spring 2013: Fabrication of Composites
Professor John Nastasi and Rachel Vroman
w/ Mary Stuckert, Chana Houzi, Ryan Osterson and Adam Novack.
Following a study of the implementation of composites (lightweight structural strength achieved through the infusion of a fabric core with a plastic resin, usually fiberglass or carbon fiber) across different scales, from architecture to sailboats down to furniture, our team decided to re-engineer the icon of composite furniture, the Eames chair.

Test panel, left. Chair form, right.
Corrugated Shell Form
Structurally sound and light as is, this project sought to push the design of the Eames chair to its limits by reducing the material to it most efficient form. The resulting chair replaced the original Eames fabrication process of manual composite layup with shredded fiberglass mat, a thicker core, with a thinner bi-axial fiberglass mat in a corrugated form that goes through the process of vacuum resin infusion, resulting in an incredibly thin and light yet supportive shell chair.
