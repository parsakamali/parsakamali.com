Confrontations : Rubell Family Collection
Working with the country’s largest private contemporary art collectors, Mera and Don Rubell, this project seeks to imagine new ways in which private art collections engage the public and questions the very definition of “public” and the role of the contemporary museum in relation to it.

Fall 2013: Rubell Family Collection
Critic: Florian Idenburg
Set inside an abandoned middle school in Washington DC that the Rubells had purchased, this scheme proposes leaving the decrepit buildings as they are and surgically inserting 35,000sf of museum inside it. Over time the school is allowed to simply decay and expose the museum on its interior. The entire museum measures 8' wide. This decision is a direct response to our aforementioned flattened consumption of art, thus forcing the observer to be put in direct confrontation with the art itself, disallowing space to be taken for granted.
